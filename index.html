About Me

I'm Rick Yanashita, a software developer and computer science student studying at Williams College! I'm interested in programming languages, compliers, internal tools, and of course UI/UX design. There is something satisfying about designing simple, visually pleasing, and widely accessible interfaces. Beyond computer science, I'm an avid jazz listener, late night hangout enthusiast, and a NCAA D3 cross country & track and field athlete.

I grew up in Tokyo, Japan, and moved to California when I was 12. Due to this and many other experiences in my life, I'm well adapted to change. I'm currently studying at Williams College, which is on the other side of the nation from California. After graduation, I have no idea where I'll be, but I know I'll be able to adapt and thrive in my new environment.

Design Sprints

Needfinding

In the first design sprint, I executed a round of needfinding to explore fitness watches as a personal health device. The most important measure of human performance for fitness watches is sensory-motor skills, especially in this context of runners. I conducted interviews with three people. An interesting insight from this round of needfinding is that there are polarizing opinions on the haptic feedback

Designing for others

In the second design sprint, my team and I redesigned sarah.williams.edu for mobile interfaces, designing specifically for students with jobs. We made sketches, paper prototypes, a mood board, and the final Figma prototype. We kept in mind Gestalt principles during the whole sprint.

Designing for interactivity

In the third design sprint, my team and I redesigned the controls for Super Mario Bros. We remapped the game's controls from keyboard to gestures using OpenCV and MediaPipe's Gesture Recognizer. This design sprint was a ton of fun!
